 History

I graduated from Stanford Lake College at the end of 2011 and was fortunate enough to be able to take a GAP year at a school in Switzerland. I spent most of 2012 in Switzerland where I made sure to use every opportunity that presented itself to me. I particularly enjoyed my involvement with the students during the winter sports program as well as the summer expeditions.

On returning to South Africa, I worked for a short while as a warehouse manager in Polokwane and then later as an adventure facilitator at Stanford Lake College and then at the Warriors Adventure Academy.

I have passed the FGASA level 1 course and completed the practical component of my field guide training course at Umlani Bush Camp through EcoTraining in July 2016. I was able to work on a free-lance basis for a short time in the Timbavati.

I was employed as a field guide at Kololo Reserve (which is adjacent to the Welgevonden Reserve) from January 2017 until September 2018. I  completed a trails guide course offered by Lowveld Trails in the Hoedspruit area and have spent two months as a volunteer at Phezulu Treehouse Lodge in Guernsey in order to collect hours towards my Lead Trails Qualification

I have also registered for my FGASA Level II and have begun the process of completing this qualification and will be doing my lead trail assessment before the end of 2019.

Although I am quiet by nature, I have used the time away from home to develop my people skills and in particular, my interaction with adults and my peers and feel that these efforts have been successful. I have a passion for the outdoors and will follow a career in this field.
